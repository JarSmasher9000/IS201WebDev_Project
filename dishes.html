Rich flavours Dishes of Japan

The Japanese people take pride in their home culinary arts and tastes. However, current Japanese food industries also aren't afraid to expand and implement other cultures foods. Some of these expansions came via foreigners coming to the country and opening up authentic restraunts from their home cultures. Popular implemented dishes from other countries include: India, China, Sri Lanka, United States, Korea, France, and Italia to name a few.


The above photos are examples of a Japanese original dishes. The left photo is a dish called 御節 (Osechi) eaten as a New Year's festive dish. It contains fish, pickled and/or cooked veggies, other seafood, sweets, beans, and more in an organized and sort of satisfying bento box.


The second photo above is a good example of another traditional Japanese dish. This dish's categorization falls under 和食 (washoku) or Japanese cooking or traditional Japanese food. These types of dishes typically include: tempura, sashimi, miso soup, rice, pickled veggies, soy sauce, and boiled and/or cooked meats.
